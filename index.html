Welcome to Zoe's website!

Hi~I am Lin Zhou. I am an experimental psychologist, doing research in psycholinguistics with behavioral measures and EEG techniques. I did my PhD in cognitive psychology from Department of Psychology at University of Pittsburgh, where I was advised by Charles Perfetti in the Learning Research and Development Center. During my PhD, I completed the graduate training program with a certificate in neuroscience from Center of Neural Basis of Cognition, a joint center of the University of Pittsburgh and Carnegie Mellon University.

Research projects

Novel word learning

How are new words integrated into language learners’ semantic memory? Is integration related to how well the learners remember the words?

  • More

Cross-cultural universals in word identification

Written Chinese, a morpho-syllabic writing system, presents a sharp contrast to alphabetic writing. Is there a universal way of characterizing the orthography-to-phonology mappings of all written languages?

  • More

Sublexical processing during Chinese reading

Most Chinese characters are phonograms (e.g., 弥), which consist of a semantic radical (e.g., 弓) and a phonetic radicals (e..g, 尔). How are these sub-lexical units (i.e., radicals) processed during word identification?

  • More

Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) for Chinese text input

The BCI project for Chinese text input was my first research experience at The Chinese University of Hong Kong. It aimed to help Chinese ALS patients to type in Chinese characters with the brain waves.
